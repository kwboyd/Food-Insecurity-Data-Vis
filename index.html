Food Security in the U.S.

By KATE BOYD

OCTOBER 28, 2015

Note: This piece was created for educational purposes only. Neithor this site nor the author are affiliated with LA Times, and this is not an LA Times story. This site uses the LA Times CSS stylesheet and elements of the layout, which belong to the LA Times.

More than one in ten Americans qualify as "food insecure." Those who are food secure are certain of their ability to purchase balanced meals at all times of the year, while those experiencing food insecurity regularly worry that their food will run out. These charts illustrate which Americans are struggling to fill their plates.

Food Insecurity by State

Percent of households in each state averaged from 2012 through 2014.

National Average: 14.3%

The United States Department of Agriculture defines food insecurity as "limited or uncertain availability of nutritionally adequate and safe foods."

5.6% of Americans qualify as having very low food insecurity, meaning they are likely to regularly eat less than they should due to lack of funds.
